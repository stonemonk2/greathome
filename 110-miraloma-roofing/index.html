Section 1

Roofing System Primer

The four bids propose three different roofing systems. Here is what each one is written in plain language, and what living with it looks like.

SPF Foam

Spray polyurethane foam · proposed by Armstrong

Overview

Spray-applied liquid foam that expands and cures into a seamless, monolithic layer. It is self-flashing — the foam conforms to and seals around penetrations, walls, and transitions without separate flashing details.

Benefits

  • No seams, so there are no joints to fail.
  • Excellent insulation value (R-6.5 per inch).
  • Lightweight.
  • Foam can last forever if top coat is maintained. Top coat can last 25-30yrs. Also, "replacement" isn't the foam base, but only a recoating of topcoats (20% of new roof cost)
  • Repairs are simple: patch and recoat.

Challenges & Maintenance

  • The UV-protective coating must be reapplied roughly every 10–15 years, or the foam degrades (per general internet searches). Armstrong says their modern topcoats actually last up to 25-30 years.
  • Fewer specialized contractors work with SPF.
  • Adhesion quality depends heavily on installer skill and surface prep.

TPO

Thermoplastic polyolefin · proposed by Sidex and NC Roofing

Overview

Single-ply synthetic membrane sheets installed over insulation board, with the seams heat-welded together.

Benefits

  • 20–30 year typical lifespan.
  • Reflective and energy efficient.
  • Contractors are widely available.
  • Lower upfront maintenance than foam.

Challenges & Maintenance

  • Seams are the weak point — quality depends on weld integrity.
  • No self-healing if a seam fails.
  • The membrane can be punctured by foot traffic or debris.

Modified Bitumen / Torch Down

Polyglass · proposed by Premier Roofing & Retro-Fit

Overview

Asphalt-based multi-ply membrane, heat-torched (fused) to the roof deck. A traditional, well-proven flat-roof system — this is what is currently on the property.

Benefits

  • Long track record.
  • Granule surface resists punctures and UV.
  • Warranties up to 20–30 years depending on system tier.
  • Familiar upgrade path from the existing roof.

Challenges & Maintenance

  • Installation involves open flame, so there is fire risk during install.
  • Heavier than TPO or foam.
  • Ponding water resistance is decent but not exceptional.

Section 3

Key Open Questions & Tradeoffs

  • Armstrong — lowest price and longest track record. Payment terms, offer 1 year interest free with their partner Regions Bank, or $1000 deposit, then pay after job is finished. Foam can last forever if top coat is maintained. Top coat can last 25-30yrs. Also, replacement is recoating of topcoats (20% of new roof cost).
  • Sidex — mid-price and the most responsive to questions, but the weakest workmanship warranty (5 years).
  • NC Roofing — most expensive with the strongest paper warranty, but has documented complaints worth weighing.
  • Premier — matches the current roof type with a strong 20-year transferable warranty, but the tightest, most contractor-favorable payment and cancellation terms of the four.
